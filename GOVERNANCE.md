# Project Governance

## Principles

Reconciliation Through Relationships is maintained openly, with decisions guided by participant safety, Indigenous data sovereignty, accessibility, privacy, maintainability, and the project's stated mission. Technical convenience does not override these responsibilities.

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who submits an issue, design, documentation, code, review, or other project improvement is a contributor. Contributions must follow [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) and the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

### Maintainers

Maintainers triage issues, review and merge changes, manage releases, moderate project spaces, and steward security and community standards. The repository owner, [Quinntyne Brown](https://github.com/QuinntyneBrown), is the current lead maintainer.

## Decision making

Day-to-day decisions are made through issues and pull requests. Maintainers seek rough consensus, using documented product requirements, test evidence, architecture decisions, and feedback from affected communities. The lead maintainer makes the final repository decision when consensus cannot be reached.

Changes affecting Indigenous knowledge, participant data, safeguarding, privacy, or the public representation of Reconciliation Through Relationships require explicit review from an authorized project stakeholder before production use.

Significant technical or product decisions should be recorded in [docs/DECISIONS.md](docs/DECISIONS.md) or an architecture decision record. Security-sensitive decisions may remain private.

## Becoming a maintainer

Maintainers may invite contributors who demonstrate sustained, constructive participation; sound technical and community judgment; reliable reviews; and consistent adherence to project values. Maintainer access may be removed for inactivity, security reasons, or violations of project policy after reasonable notice when circumstances permit.

## Changes to governance

Governance changes are proposed and reviewed through a pull request. Material changes should remain open long enough for active contributors and affected stakeholders to respond.
