# Reconciliation Through Relationships (RTR) matching platform: Product Notes

## Product Goal

Create a simple, welcoming portal that helps Indigenous and non-Indigenous participants complete onboarding, prepare for participation, build relationships, and form regional reconciliation cohorts.

The system should be straightforward for non-technical staff to operate and maintain.

## Website Integration

- Determine how the portal will connect to or be embedded in RTR's existing website.
- Maintain consistent branding and provide a clear transition between the public website and the participant portal.
- Confirm whether existing website accounts, content, or services need to be integrated.

## User Roles

The current notes suggest the following roles:

- Participant
- Facilitator
- Administrator

The permissions and responsibilities for each role still need to be confirmed.

## Authentication

Possible sign-in methods include:

- Magic-link authentication
- Email and password

One method should be selected for the initial version.

## Participant Onboarding

The onboarding process should collect the information needed to create useful profiles and make appropriate match recommendations.

Suggested profile fields include:

- Name
- Biography
- Sex, with an option to specify
- Language preferences
- Location:
  - City, town, or county
  - Province
- Nearest treaty area to where the participant lives, worships, or works. The Native Land Digital place search at https://native-land.ca/ may be used for assistance.
- Participation category (select one or more):
  - Indigenous leader
  - Indigenous individual
  - Non-Indigenous individual
  - Elected leader
  - Religious leader
  - Artist
- Faith tradition
- Ministries, causes, hobbies, or interests the participant is passionate about
- Availability and preferred ways to connect
- Personal boundaries and matching preferences

At least one of the participation categories identified as mandatory by RTR must be selected.

## Learning Journey

Participants should complete required learning content before they become eligible for matching or cohort participation.

The learning system should:

- Present RTR's existing training videos and related materials.
- Track each participant's progress and completion status.
- Send reminders to participants who have not completed onboarding or required learning.
- Show facilitators and administrators which participants are eligible to proceed.

## Participant Dashboard

The main participant dashboard should provide clear navigation to profiles, learning content, matches, messages, and events.

Potential participant discovery filters include:

- Indigenous participants
- Non-Indigenous participants
- All eligible participants
- Recommended connections, with up to five suggestions

The dashboard could display a cohort-readiness indicator when at least five eligible participants are located in the same region. RTR must confirm the required number and geographic boundaries.

## Matching and Connection Process

The matching system should recommend suitable Indigenous and non-Indigenous connections based on factors such as:

- Geographic proximity
- Shared interests or ministry involvement
- Language
- Faith tradition
- Availability
- Preferred connection format
- Personal boundaries

Suggested connection process:

1. The system recommends a potential connection.
2. A facilitator reviews and approves the recommendation.
3. Each participant reviews the other participant's profile.
4. Both participants choose whether to connect.
5. If both agree, they can schedule an introductory call and begin messaging.

## Participant Profile Page

A participant should be able to:

- View and update their own profile.
- Review an approved potential connection's profile.
- Accept or decline a connection request.
- Schedule an introductory call after both participants agree to connect.
- Exchange messages with an approved connection.

Call recording or transcription should only be considered after RTR confirms the consent, privacy, safeguarding, storage, and access requirements.

## Events and Regional Cohorts

Authorized users should be able to create and manage events for regional reconciliation cohorts.

Potential capabilities include:

- Create and edit an event.
- Invite eligible participants in a region.
- Track registrations or attendance.
- Notify participants about event updates.

## Facilitator and Administrator Pages

Facilitators and administrators may need tools to:

- Review and approve participant registrations.
- Monitor onboarding and learning progress.
- Review and approve suggested matches.
- Create or adjust a match manually.
- Manage regional cohorts and events.
- Send reminders or other participant communications.
- Respond to safeguarding, privacy, or support concerns.

## Design Goals

- Keep the participant experience welcoming, accessible, and easy to navigate.
- Make routine content and participant management simple for non-technical staff.
- Protect participants' personal information and precise locations.
- Require clear consent before sharing profile information or enabling communication.
- Use respectful, culturally appropriate language throughout the platform.
- Support mobile and desktop devices.

## Decisions to Confirm with RTR

- How should the portal integrate with RTR's existing website?
- Which sign-in method should be used?
- What are the final user roles and permissions?
- Which profile fields and participation categories are mandatory?
- How should faith, ministry interests, or spiritual interests influence matching?
- Can participants browse profiles, or should they see only facilitator-approved recommendations?
- What must a participant complete before becoming eligible for matching?
- How should regional proximity be calculated without exposing precise locations?
- How many participants are required to form a regional cohort?
- Which users may create and manage events?
- Are in-platform messaging and call scheduling required for the initial version?
- Should calls ever be recorded or transcribed? If so, what consent and data-protection rules apply?
- Which email reminders and notifications should be automated?
