Design system & component library

Every component in the built application, in one place. Each specimen uses the same classes the app ships (rtr.css mirrors src/app/globals.css and src/components/ui/*). The direction is “two strands, one circle” — warm, land-grounded, and built for readability first.

Principles

Welcome before wow

Participants range widely in age and comfort with technology. Large type, plain words, one clear action per screen.

Consent is a design element

Nothing about a person is shown — no photo, no precise location — until they choose to share it. Avatars use initials; the map shows regions, not addresses.

Abstract, not appropriated

Motifs (woven strands, the circle) are geometric abstractions of relationship and gathering. Final iconography must be reviewed with RTR’s Indigenous leadership.

Color

Drawn from the land: spruce forest, birchbark, prairie ochre, river water, wild berry. Spruce carries structure and action; ochre and river are the two relationship strands; berry is reserved for warnings. Token values live in src/styles/design-tokens.css and are shared with the app.

Brand & motifs

The mark

BrandMark — two arcs, ochre and river, forming one circle around two people. Open at top and bottom: the relationship is never closed to others.

The muted mark

BrandMark muted — birch-grey, dots removed, one strand dashed. Used inside empty states while a circle is still waiting to form.

The woven strand

Weave — two strands interlacing, used as a divider and under page titles. Once per view, never as wallpaper. An on-dark variant lightens both strands for spruce panels.

Panel-on-dark motif

.rtr-panel-on-dark lays a faint figure — two facing profile silhouettes joined by an open circle — behind spruce-800/900 surfaces at 5% opacity. It appears on the landing hero, the signup aside, and the footer. Tune per panel with --rtr-figure-position / --rtr-figure-size.

Cohort circle

The signature component (CohortCircle). Each seat is an eligible participant in a region. When the circle reaches its threshold, it turns ochre and gains its gathering ring — the region is ready to form a reconciliation cohort. The threshold is configurable per deployment; the facilitator settings page exposes a range of 3–20 (default 5, shown here).

On dark

on-dark — ochre-500 fill, spruce-900 text. The primary call to action on spruce headers and hero panels.

Rules: one primary button per view. Buttons name the action’s result — “Schedule call,” not “Submit.” Reversible refusals use the quiet danger outline; solid berry (destructive) is reserved for hard-to-undo actions.

Accessibility commitments

✓ Body face is Atkinson Hyperlegible, designed for low-vision readers.

✓ Text and interactive colors meet WCAG AA contrast on their backgrounds.

✓ Every interactive element shows a visible ochre focus ring.

✓ Touch targets are at least 44px; base text is 16.5px.

✓ prefers-reduced-motion disables all transitions.

✓ Status is never conveyed by color alone — pills carry text, seats carry counts.

✓ Layouts collapse to a single column below 620px; nav collapses at 860px.

✓ Plain language throughout; reading level checked with RTR staff.
