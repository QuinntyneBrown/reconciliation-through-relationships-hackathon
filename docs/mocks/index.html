Reconciliation Through Relationships

Design mocks

A faithful catalog of the built platform — every page, overlay, and transient state, rendered in the app’s own components. Direction: “two strands, one circle” — spruce and birch from the land, ochre and river as the two relationship strands, the cohort circle as the signature element. All names and data are synthetic.

Design system

Component libraryEvery implemented component in one document — color, type, buttons, badges, forms, avatars, cards, tabs, alerts, toasts, dialogs, notifications, cohort circle, and accessibility commitments. Onboarding validation patternInteractive initial, submitted-invalid, partially corrected, and valid states for the onboarding steps that validate on submit.

Onboarding

04Step 1 · Basic infoName, age, bio, whether you’re Indigenous, sex, and participation-category cards. 04aStep 1 · Submitted invalidThe error summary (“Please fix 6 errors to continue”) with anchor links, and inline field errors on every invalid control. 05Step 2 · LocationCity, province or territory, treaty area, and a link out to native-land.ca. 06Step 3 · Faith & interestsFaith-tradition radio cards and a free-form interest tag input. 07Step 4 · AvailabilityDay, time, format, and language preference cards. 08Step 5 · MatchingPreference switches, a personal-boundaries note, the map-consent card, and Complete profile.

Notes for the team

Sources

These mocks now mirror the implemented application. Ground truth is src/app and src/components; the shared rtr.css imports the same design tokens the app ships (src/styles/design-tokens.css). Every user-facing string is copied from source, not paraphrased. When a screen changes in the app, update its mock.

Cultural review

All motifs are deliberate geometric abstractions (woven strands, an open circle). Before launch, review iconography, language, and synthetic content with RTR’s Indigenous leadership and Joel Gordon.
