ABOUT RTR

Reconciliation Through Relationships was launched in response to the Truth and Reconciliation Commission's call to bring Indigenous and non-Indigenous people together through meaningful conversations and interactions.

RTR is led by Andrew Russell of Wood Mountain Lakota First Nation, who serves as the organization's Indigenous ambassador and partnership liaison.

The initiative was founded in 2021 by Indigenous leaders Jimmy Thunder of Reconciliation Thunder and Ji-gaabiikwe (Diane Campeau) of iEmergence, together with Joel Gordon, a non-Indigenous convenor from The Evangelical Fellowship of Canada (EFC).


THE CHALLENGE

RTR needs a welcoming and culturally appropriate digital platform that helps participants move from initial registration to learning, relationship-building, and local action.

The platform should help RTR:

1. Recruit and onboard new Indigenous and non-Indigenous participants.
2. Automate participant registration and the learning journey using RTR's existing training videos.
3. Help facilitators identify participants in the same geographic region and support the formation of local reconciliation cohorts.
4. Assist facilitators in matching Indigenous and non-Indigenous participants for relationship-building.


PROPOSED SOLUTION

Build a responsive web application that supports the participant journey described below. For the hackathon, focus on the primary end-to-end experience, use synthetic participant data, and implement lightweight authentication.


1. Account Creation and Participant Profile

Provide a structured and welcoming onboarding process. Participants should be able to create an account and enter the following information:

- Name
- Sex, with an option to specify
- Participation category (select one or more):
  - Indigenous leader
  - Indigenous individual
  - Non-Indigenous individual
  - Elected leader
  - Religious leader
  - Artist
- Location:
  - City, town, or county
  - Province
- Nearest treaty area to where the participant lives, worships, or works. The Native Land Digital place search at https://native-land.ca/ may be used for assistance.
- Faith tradition:
  - Indigenous Traditional
  - Atheist
  - Christian
  - Jewish
  - Muslim
  - Hindu
  - Buddhist
  - Other, with an option to specify
- Hobbies and interests, entered as keywords or tags
- Availability
- Preferred participation format
- Language preferences
- Personal boundaries and other matching preferences

At least one of the required participation categories identified by RTR must be selected.


2. Learning Journey

Registered participants must complete a learning journey before engaging with other participants. The platform should use RTR's existing training videos to guide participants through this preparation and track their progress.

Participants should appear as eligible for mapping, cohort formation, or matching only after completing the required learning journey.


3. Regional Participant Map and Reconciliation Cohorts

With appropriate consent and privacy safeguards, participants who have completed the learning journey may be represented on a regional map. The map should help authorized facilitators identify areas with enough participants to form a reconciliation cohort.

A regional cohort could collaborate on local reconciliation events, such as:

- A film screening on June 21, National Indigenous Peoples Day
- A blanket exercise on September 30, the National Day for Truth and Reconciliation

The initial suggestion is to form a cohort when at least five eligible participants are present in a region. RTR must confirm this threshold.


4. Participant Matching

The platform should suggest viable Indigenous and non-Indigenous participant matches based on factors such as:

- Geographic location
- Availability
- Language
- Shared interests
- Faith tradition
- Participation format and personal boundaries

A facilitator must review and approve suggested matches before participants are connected.

The matching process must also account for an imbalance in registrations. If more non-Indigenous participants register than can be matched, the platform should provide a waitlist or a learning-resources-only pathway.


HACKATHON SCOPE

The hackathon solution should include:

- A responsive web application
- The agreed-upon user roles and permissions
- The primary end-to-end participant journey
- Participant profiles and intake
- Learning journey progress tracking
- Facilitator-supported matching
- Regional participant discovery and cohort support
- Synthetic demonstration data
- Lightweight authentication, such as a magic link or a simple email-and-password login


AVAILABLE RESOURCES

- Joel Gordon of EFC will be the on-site domain steward for vision, partnerships, and communications. He is not the technical lead; consult him on cultural context and requirements.
- Technical mentors will be available to provide architecture and technology support.
- RTR has access to national networks of Christian leaders, churches, and schools.
- Financial resources are available to operate a server and a DigitalOcean account.
- Funding opportunities may be available to support the platform's long-term sustainability.


DECISIONS TO CONFIRM WITH RTR

- Which participation categories are mandatory during registration?
- What are the three user roles, and what permissions does each role require?
- Should the application use magic-link authentication or email and password?
- What participant information may be shown on the regional map, and to whom?
- What consent, privacy, safeguarding, and data-retention requirements apply?
- How many eligible participants are required to form a regional cohort?
- Which learning materials are mandatory, and what constitutes completion?
- How should facilitators manage waitlisted participants and approve matches?
