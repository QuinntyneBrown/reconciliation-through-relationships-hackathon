# 9. Questions and help

[← Back to contents](README.md)

Short, plain answers to the questions people ask most.

---

### I can't sign in.

Double-check that you're using the **same email** you signed up with, and that
your **password** is typed correctly. Use the little **eye icon** to see your
password as you type. If it still won't work, ask an RTR facilitator for help.

### I forgot my password.

Reach out to an **RTR facilitator** — they can help you get back into your
account.

### Do I have to finish the learning journey first?

Yes. You can only be matched, appear on the map, or join a local group **after**
you complete the learning journey. This makes sure everyone starts from a shared
understanding. See [The learning journey](03-the-learning-journey.md).

### I don't see any recommendations yet. Did I do something wrong?

No — this is normal. A facilitator reviews possible matches **personally**, so it
can take a little time. You might also be **waiting for a good match** to join.
In the meantime, browse the community under **All participants** and keep your
profile up to date. See [Your dashboard](04-your-dashboard.md).

### What is a "waitlist"?

Sometimes more non-Indigenous participants join than there are Indigenous
participants to match with. If that happens, you may be placed on a **waitlist**.
It simply means "we'll let you know the moment there's a good match for you." You
can keep learning and exploring while you wait.

### What is a "cohort"?

A **cohort** is a small local group of RTR participants who gather for events in
their community, such as a film screening or a blanket exercise. See [The
regional map](06-the-regional-map.md).

### What is a "treaty area"?

It's the treaty territory nearest to where you live, worship, or work. If you're
not sure which one, the sign-up form links to **native-land.ca**, a website that
helps you look it up. This field is optional.

### Who can see my information?

Only **your matched connection** and **RTR facilitators** can see your profile
details. Other participants see your public profile, but **never** your email or
your exact address. See [Your profile and privacy](07-your-profile-and-privacy.md).

### Who can see where I live?

Only your **city** is ever shown, and only if you chose to appear on the map.
Your exact address is never shown to anyone. You can change your map choice with
help from a facilitator.

### How do I set up a video call?

Open a conversation with your connection and click **Schedule call**. Choose a
time and details, and your connection will be notified. Video calls happen
through **Zoom**. See [Connecting and messaging](05-connecting-and-messaging.md).

### What does a facilitator do?

Facilitators are trained people who guide the community. They review and approve
every match, keep an eye on participants' progress, and help local groups form.
See [For facilitators](08-for-facilitators.md).

### Are the people in the pictures in this guide real?

No. Every name, face, and message in the screenshots is **made-up demonstration
data**, used only to show how the app looks.

### A page didn't load properly.

Try refreshing the page in your browser. If a problem keeps happening, let an RTR
facilitator know.
